Octavia Butler

Roots

Butler was born in Pasadena, CA on June 22nd, 1947 and lived most of her life in Southern California. She passed away in Lake Forest Park, Washington on February 24th, 2006.

What I Admire about Butler

Dedication to her craft~science fiction writing, her captivating stories, the extensive body of work she's left behind for the world, the lessons embedded in them, the wisdom she's given the world outside of her work! Truly a brilliant legacy!

Creations and Accomplishments

Butler's work has shown to inspire many creatives and activists in their respective works. She's also widley known for the prolific nature of her work. Some of her many accomplishments include:

  • 15 novels and short story collections, Kindred and Parable of the Sower (series) being the most renowned.
  • 6 novels, including the Macarthur Fellowship and Hugo Award for Best Novelette.

"First forget inspiration. Habit is more dependable. Habit will sustain you whether you're inspired or not. Habit will help you finish and polish your stories. Inspiration won't. Habit is persitence in practice."

--Octavia Butler
